About the project

The QDG initiative -
Qatar’s digital leap

The Ministry of Transport and Communications (MOTC), based in Doha, is pivotal in developing and implementing Qatar's transport and communication infrastructure.

Aligned with Qatar's vision for a knowledge-based economy, MOTC has launched the Qatar Digital Government (QDG) initiative to digitize government services and improve their accessibility to citizens and residents in Qatar.

What we had in mind

Trust, reliability, and accessibility

Amidst the challenges of the COVID-19 pandemic and a regional political blockade, MOTC planned to launch QDG to minimize physical visits to governmental offices.

In partnership with Accenture, a strategic plan was devised to position QDG as a trusted and reliable brand in the eyes of residents and citizens.

The goal: transform the government into a digital entity, fostering trust and forging an unbroken connection with the people.

The need for a strategic approach

To create a strong brand identity for QDG, ORIGIN prioritized understanding the audience through comprehensive market research. This approach ensured the platform, designed with a focus on user experience, meets the needs of Qatar’s residents, establishing itself as a trusted brand in the digital government realm.

How we made it happen

Crunching the numbers

Our quantitative research sought numerical insights into the attitudes and behaviors of Qatari citizens and residents regarding digital government services, revealing key needs and pain points.

Voices from Qatar

Our qualitative research used in-depth interviews and focus groups to delve into the perceptions, opinions, and experiences of Qatari citizens and residents with digital government services.

Brand Building

Brand rationale – This is my Qatar

Recognizing the significant influence a brand name holds as the initial impression, our focus was on instilling a sense of belonging and a strong connection to home through the Hero archetype. Thus, we curated a relatable and personalized name, 'Qatar-e,' aiming to embody the heroic essence while fostering a profound connection to Qatar as a homeland. The simple, memorable name, meaning 'My Qatar' in Arabic, reflects pride and ownership. It humanizes the brand, emphasizing accessibility and approachability in the digital government space.

The Arabic logo incorporates the Arabic letter إي (pronounced "ee"), which means "yes." This addition gives a positive and optimistic connotation to the word "Qatar" that precedes it, creating a sense of agreement, confirmation, and pride.

Brand Building

A state of mind, a vision of the future

Launching the platform required a campaign that resonates with the fast-paced, highly digitized, and time-conscious society. Targeting the youthful demographic, our campaigns aimed to provide a modern, creative representation mirroring their aspirational lifestyle.

Qatar-e encapsulates this vision, embodying a positive outlook and saying 'YES' to the future

The campaign for Qatar-e focuses on the present, emphasizing the immediate availability of online government services in Qatar's fast-paced, digitized society, highlighting time-saving benefits and fostering a sense of community through messages like "Join the movement."

Our vision’s outcome
